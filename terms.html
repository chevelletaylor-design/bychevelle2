1. Deposit & payment.

All packages require a 50% non-refundable deposit to secure your project slot. The deposit is due before the Strategy Call is scheduled, and the remaining 50% is due before the site goes live.

Invoices are sent via Stripe or direct bank transfer. Payment must be received before the corresponding work begins — meaning the Strategy Call doesn't get scheduled until the deposit is paid, and the site doesn't go live until the final 50% is paid.

Why non-refundable

The deposit holds your spot in my schedule and covers the strategy, planning, and front-loaded creative work that begins immediately upon payment.

2. Asset delivery.

All photos, copy notes, logo files, and brand materials must be uploaded to one shared Google Drive folder before the Strategy Call. I'll create the folder and share access with you when you book your project.

Everything goes in the Drive folder — that way nothing gets lost, nothing gets forgotten, and we both have a clean record of what was provided.

The 3-week build window starts once your deposit and all assets are received.

3. Timeline & review windows.

A typical project follows this timeline once the deposit and assets are received:

  • Weeks 1–3: I build the site
  • Days 1–7 after delivery: Your first review window
  • Weeks 4–6: I apply revisions
  • Days 1–7 after revisions: Your second review window
  • Week 7: Final edits, handoff, and launch

Total turnaround is approximately 6–7 weeks from deposit to launch, assuming you stay within your review windows.

The Operator package and other complex builds may have longer timelines — these are scoped after the Strategy Call.

4. Revision rounds.

Every package includes two rounds of revisions:

Round 1 — Structural. This is the round for big-picture changes: section reordering, copy direction, layout shifts, photo swaps, page restructuring. Tell me everything you want changed in this round.

Round 2 — Refinement. This round is for polish only: typos, alignment tweaks, photo refinements, and small copy tweaks. Round 2 is not for major redesigns.

Important

If Round 2 feedback includes major redesign requests (rebuilding sections, changing the overall direction, swapping core layouts), that's considered a new scope and will be quoted separately at $150/hr with a 1-hour minimum, or as an additional revision round at $150 flat.

Additional revision rounds beyond the two included can be purchased at $150 per round through The Extension.

5. Inactive projects & restart fees.

If you don't respond within your review windows and I don't hear from you, the project becomes inactive. Restart fees apply based on how long the project has been stale:

  • 1–30 days inactive: $250 restart fee. Work resumes once I have an open spot in my schedule.
  • 31–60 days inactive: $500 restart fee, plus a scope re-review (pricing may need to be adjusted if the project has materially changed).
  • 61+ days inactive: Project is considered closed. Resuming requires booking as a new project at current rates, and any deposit paid does not transfer.

Restart fees are in addition to your remaining package balance. They cover the time it takes me to re-immerse in your project and fit it back into my schedule.

Avoiding restart fees

If you know you're going to need more time on a review, just tell me before your window closes. I'll always work with you on extended timelines if we plan it together. Restart fees only apply when communication goes silent.

6. Project queue priority.

I work on multiple projects at a time, and each active client occupies a priority slot in my schedule.

If you exceed your review window without communication, your project goes inactive and your priority slot opens up for the next active client. When you come back, I'll work you in around what's already booked, which means turnaround on later revisions extends based on what's in my schedule at the time.

This is just the reality of fitting your project around what's already booked. It's not personal — it's how I keep timelines workable for everyone.

7. Refund policy.

The 50% deposit is non-refundable under any circumstance once paid. The deposit covers strategy, planning, and front-loaded creative work that begins immediately upon payment.

The final 50% is due before launch and is also non-refundable once paid.

If you decide to cancel the project after the deposit is paid but before the Strategy Call, no further work happens but the deposit is not returned. If you cancel after the Strategy Call or during the build phase, the deposit covers the work completed to that point.

8. Ownership & intellectual property.

Once the final invoice is paid, you own the website I built for you, including all custom design, custom copy, and any logo work I created specifically for your project.

Photos, fonts, and any third-party assets used in the site retain their original licensing. I'll let you know during the build if any asset has restrictions you need to be aware of.

I retain the right to display your finished site in my portfolio and on social media unless we agree otherwise in writing before the project starts.

Until the final invoice is paid, all work is the property of By Chevelle.

9. Hosting & domain.

I do not host your site for you. Your site lives on your own Netlify account, which I'll set up with you during the project. Most clients can use Netlify's free tier; I'll advise you if your traffic suggests a paid plan would be needed.

Your domain name needs to be purchased separately by you (Namecheap, GoDaddy, Google Domains, etc.). I'll walk you through connecting it to your Netlify deploy during handoff.

I do not build sites on Squarespace, Wix, or Shopify. Every site I build comes with a self-managed admin panel that lets you make updates yourself — exactly the kind of editing you'd be used to from those platforms. From your admin you'll be able to:

  • Swap photos and update images
  • Edit page text and section copy
  • Update banners, links, and buttons
  • Add or update testimonials and gallery items
  • Manage your portfolio entries (if applicable)
  • Update prices, services, and contact details
  • Toggle promo banners on and off

You won't be able to redesign sections, add new pages, or change layouts through the admin — those are scope changes. But everyday content updates are entirely in your hands.

10. Ongoing maintenance.

After your site launches, you're equipped to handle most updates yourself through the admin panel. For things you don't want to handle — or anything that requires me to get back into the code — I offer maintenance through The Extension:

Monthly maintenance — $200+/month. Photo swaps, link updates, copy edits, thumbnail changes, and small content adjustments. This is for keeping your site current — not for full overhauls or redesigns.

New pages or features — quoted by scope. If you want to add a new page after launch (a seasonal offer, a new service, an event landing page, etc.), that's quoted as new work — not at the $150/page in-build rate. Add-on pages added during a build are different from post-launch pages built later.

Custom updates and redesigns — quoted by scope.

I am not on retainer or on-call by default. Maintenance is opt-in.

11. Scope changes.

Your package scope is locked in after the Strategy Call. Adding pages, features, or significant new requirements after that call will require a scope adjustment and additional pricing.

Common scope additions and their pricing:

  • Additional page added mid-project: $150
  • New feature or integration mid-project: quoted by scope
  • Significant redesign mid-project: quoted by scope

12. Communication policy.

Communication during the project happens through scheduled calls and the structured Consultation and Feedback pages — not through email threads, text messages, or DMs.

Email is for confirmations, scheduled check-ins, and minor questions. It's not an open 24/7 channel.

The Consultation Quiz is where you give me the full picture of what your site needs to be. Take it seriously. If you forget to mention something during the quiz or our Strategy Call, your next chance to bring it up is during the Round 1 Feedback window. After that, anything new is treated as a scope change.

Significant additions — like a whole new page, a new feature, or a redesign request — aren't handled over text or email. Those are scope conversations that get scheduled.

Questions about anything here? Reach out before you sign.
