1. Deposit & payment.

All packages require a 50% non-refundable deposit to secure your project slot and begin work. The remaining 50% is due before the site goes live.

Invoices are sent via Stripe or direct bank transfer. Payment must clear before the corresponding work begins — meaning the build doesn't start until the deposit clears, and the site doesn't go live until the final 50% clears.

Why non-refundable

The deposit holds your spot in my schedule and covers the strategy, planning, and front-loaded creative work that happens before any visible deliverable. Once you've paid the deposit, that work starts.

2. Asset delivery.

All photos, copy notes, logo files, and brand materials must be uploaded to one shared Google Drive folder before the strategy call. I'll create the folder and share access with you when you pay your deposit.

I do not accept assets piecemeal via text, email, DMs, or any other channel. Everything goes in the Drive folder. This protects both of us — nothing gets lost, nothing gets forgotten, and we both have a record of what was provided when.

The 3-week build window starts the day after the strategy call or the day all assets are delivered, whichever is later.

3. Timeline & review windows.

A typical project follows this timeline once the deposit and assets are received:

  • Weeks 1–3: I build the site
  • Days 1–7 after delivery: Your first review window
  • Weeks 4–6: I apply revisions
  • Days 1–7 after revisions: Your second review window
  • Week 7: Final edits, handoff, and launch

Total turnaround is approximately 6–7 weeks from deposit to launch, assuming you stay within your review windows.

The Operator package and other complex builds may have longer timelines — these are scoped after the strategy call.

4. Revision rounds.

Every package includes two rounds of revisions:

Round 1 — Structural. This is the round for big-picture changes: section reordering, copy direction, layout shifts, photo swaps, page restructuring. Tell me everything you want changed in this round.

Round 2 — Refinement. This round is for polish only: typos, alignment tweaks, color adjustments, photo refinements, small copy tweaks. Round 2 is not for major redesigns.

Important

If Round 2 feedback includes major redesign requests (rebuilding sections, changing the overall direction, swapping core layouts), that's considered a new scope and will be quoted separately at $150/hr with a 1-hour minimum, or as an additional revision round at $150 flat.

Additional revision rounds beyond the two included can be purchased at $150 per round through The Extension.

5. Inactive projects & restart fees.

If you don't respond within your review windows and I don't hear from you, the project becomes inactive. Restart fees apply based on how long the project has been stale:

  • 1–30 days inactive: $250 restart fee. Work resumes when there's space in my queue.
  • 31–60 days inactive: $500 restart fee, plus a scope re-review (pricing may need to be adjusted if the project has materially changed).
  • 61+ days inactive: Project is considered closed. Resuming requires booking as a new project at current rates, and any deposit paid does not transfer.

Restart fees are in addition to your remaining package balance. They cover the time it takes me to re-immerse in your project, re-prioritize the queue, and pick up where we left off.

Avoiding restart fees

If you know you're going to need more time on a review, just tell me before your window closes. I'll always work with you on extended timelines if we plan it together. Restart fees only apply when communication goes silent.

6. Project queue priority.

I work on multiple projects at once, with each client occupying a priority slot in my queue based on their project status.

Active clients who stay within their review windows hold their priority slot. Clients who exceed their review windows without communication lose their slot, and turnaround on subsequent revisions extends based on what else is in my queue at the time.

This is how I'm able to keep my pricing accessible and my work quality high — every client who respects the timeline gets my full attention during their build window.

7. Refund policy.

The 50% deposit is non-refundable under any circumstance once paid. The deposit covers strategy, planning, and front-loaded creative work that begins immediately upon payment.

The final 50% is due before launch and is also non-refundable once paid.

If you decide to cancel the project after the deposit is paid but before the strategy call, no further work happens but the deposit is not returned. If you cancel after the strategy call or during the build phase, the deposit covers the work completed to that point.

8. Ownership & intellectual property.

Once the final invoice is paid, you own the website I built for you, including all custom design, custom copy, and any logo work I created specifically for your project.

Photos, fonts, and any third-party assets used in the site retain their original licensing. I'll let you know during the build if any asset has restrictions you need to be aware of.

I retain the right to display your finished site in my portfolio and on social media unless we agree otherwise in writing before the project starts.

Until the final invoice is paid, all work is the property of By Chevelle.

9. Hosting & domain.

I do not host your site for you. All sites I build are deployed to your own Netlify account, which I'll set up with you during the project. Most clients can use Netlify's free tier; I'll advise you if your traffic suggests a paid plan would be needed.

Your domain name needs to be purchased separately by you (Namecheap, GoDaddy, Google Domains, etc.). I'll walk you through connecting it to your Netlify deploy during handoff.

I do not build sites on Squarespace, Wix, or Shopify. All sites are built custom on a static-site architecture with a self-managed admin panel where applicable.

10. Ongoing maintenance.

After your site launches, you're equipped to handle photo swaps, link updates, and minor content changes yourself through the admin panel. I'll walk you through this during handoff.

For larger updates — adding pages, redesigning sections, swapping galleries, holiday banners, or anything that requires me to get back into the code — I offer maintenance as part of The Extension:

  • Additional pages: $150 per page
  • Monthly maintenance retainer: $200+/month
  • Custom updates: Quoted by scope

I am not on retainer or on-call by default. Maintenance is opt-in.

11. Scope changes.

Your package scope is locked in after the strategy call. Adding pages, features, or significant new requirements after that call will require a scope adjustment and additional pricing.

Common scope additions and their pricing:

  • Additional page added mid-project: $150
  • New feature or integration mid-project: quoted by scope
  • Significant redesign mid-project: quoted by scope (often it's better to finish v1 and treat the redesign as a separate engagement)

The strategy call is your chance to think through everything you want. Use it.

12. Communication policy.

Project communication happens through email and the structured Feedback and Consultation pages I provide. I do not take project decisions, asset deliveries, or revision requests via text message, DM, phone calls, or any other channel — those don't create the paper trail this work requires.

I respond to project emails within 1–2 business days during active build windows. During review windows (when the ball is in your court), I'll be checking less frequently and focused on other clients.

If something is genuinely urgent and time-sensitive, mark your email subject "URGENT" and I'll prioritize it.

Questions about anything here? Reach out before you sign.
