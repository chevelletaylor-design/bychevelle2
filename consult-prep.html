Prep for your Strategy Call.

A short guide to help you get the most out of our 60-minute deep-dive — so we walk away with a clear plan for your site.

1. The right mindset.

This is your one shot to give me the full picture of what your site needs to be. After the Strategy Call, your next chance to add things is the Round 1 Feedback window — and after that, anything new becomes a scope change with new pricing.

So come ready. Think long and hard before our call. The more clarity you bring, the better the site I'll build.

2. Know your audience.

Before the call, get specific about who your site is for:

  • Who is your ideal client or visitor?
  • What are they searching for when they land on your site?
  • What's their level of familiarity with what you do?
  • What objection or doubt are they bringing with them?
  • What do they need to see to trust you and take the next step?

3. Know your message.

If your site had to say one thing, what would it be? Come ready to talk through:

  • What you do, in one sentence
  • Who you do it for
  • What makes you different from others doing similar work
  • The #1 thing you want a visitor to do after seeing your site

4. Think through structure.

You've already filled out the Consultation Quiz, which captures your top-level direction. Use the time before our call to think more concretely about:

  • Every page you want — including ones you're "not sure about"
  • What each page needs to communicate
  • Specific features (forms, galleries, video, booking, payments, etc.)
  • Anything you'd want a visitor to be able to download, sign up for, or interact with

Don't hold back. It's much easier to scope something correctly upfront than to add it later.

5. Gather references.

Pull together a small mood board before our call:

  • 3–5 sites you love (any industry — just visually inspiring)
  • 1–2 sites you specifically don't like, and what you don't like about them
  • Photos or color combinations that feel right for your brand
  • Examples of writing that sounds the way you want your site to sound

Drop these into your shared Drive folder so we can reference them during the call.

6. Decisions to come ready with.

Be ready to give me a real answer on these:

  • What's your domain name (or are you still picking one)?
  • Will you be using your own photos, stock, or a mix?
  • Do you need help writing copy, or do you have drafts?
  • What's your launch date, if any?
  • Are there integrations you need from day one (newsletter, scheduling, payments)?
  • Who else (if anyone) needs to approve the site before launch?
If you don't know yet

That's fine. Just bring your best thinking and we'll work through it together. What I want to avoid is finding out post-launch that there was a major decision we never discussed.

7. What happens after the call.

Right after our Strategy Call, I get to work. The 3-week build window starts the next day, assuming all assets are already in your shared Drive folder.

You won't hear from me much during the build — that's by design. The next time you see the site is when I send you the preview link for your First Review, where you'll have 7 days to walk through it and submit structured feedback.

Read the full process and project terms if you haven't already — they cover everything from review windows to revision rounds to what happens if a project goes inactive.

Got questions before our call? Send them ahead — I'll fold them into our agenda.
